About Me

HOW I GOT HERE

Welcome, and thanks for visiting! My name is Holly Nwangwa, and I am a proud self-made Junior Web Developer. As far as exposure to development, formally, I completed a 24-week full-stack coding bootcamp spanning from August 2017 to February 2018 at Northwestern University in Chicago. It's through this program that I exposed myself to front- and back-end development languages and some basic theories in computer science. What led me to participate in this bootcamp was my desire to better understand my full-time job at McMaster-Carr. I was working adjacent to web development, and benefitting from it, without being fully involved. The bootcamp allowed me to delve deeper and served as a stimulating learning experience. It is through this bootcamp that I had my first experience working with a team on a project with a real-life client in mind: the Museum of Contemporary Photography in Chicago. My teammates and I developed a prototype for an application the museum was interested in contracting in the future. I further go into the project - MoCP.me - in my Portfolio section above and on my resume.

Informally, I pursue side projects; mini single-page applications with simple objectives that explore programming fundamentals. Besides allowing me to practice writing functional code and honing skills I desire to be even more proficient in, these projects further expose me to the success found in web development through failure. Not many industries celebrate failure the way web development does; the unique feeling of relief after finally solving a tricky error and the excitement over encountering a brand new one is unrivaled. The more I lean into test-driven development, the more familiar I am with this attitude toward failure: that it is inevitable, rather than being a character flaw. This familiarity is crucial to staying motivated while learning in this field, and I believe I am off to a solid start. Thanks for giving me a chance, and please do not hesitate to contact me!
